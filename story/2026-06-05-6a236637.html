Bear and Papa went to Redbird just as the sky turned soft blue.

The sign glowed red. The windows looked warm. Inside, spoons clinked, shoes squeaked, and somebody laughed so hard their straw wrapper flew off the table.

Bear held Papa’s hand and bounced once.

“Redbird smells like fries and adventure,” Bear said.

Papa sniffed. “That is an accurate scientific observation.”

They sat in a booth by the window. Bear had crayons. Papa had a menu. Then Bear noticed something odd under the little metal napkin holder.

It was green.

It was square.

It had tiny feet and a worried face made of pixels.

“Papa,” Bear whispered. “There is a Minecraft creeper under the napkins.”

Papa leaned down until his light-brown eyes were level with the table. “Well, hello.”

The tiny creeper waved one blocky foot.

“I am Pip,” it squeaked. “I am a little pixel creeper. I got lost through a square hole behind the ketchup.”

Bear looked behind the ketchup.

There was, indeed, a square hole. It shimmered like a video game puddle.

Pip sniffled. “My diamond house is locked, and my key is stuck in the Redbird world. I tried to find it, but everything here is round and wobbly.”

Bear looked at Papa.

Papa looked at Bear.

There was only one choice.

“We help,” Bear said.

Papa smiled. “Team plan?”

“Team plan.”

Bear found the key first. It was not a regular key. It was a tiny diamond pickaxe charm stuck inside the crayon box. Red crayon on one side. Blue crayon on the other.

“How did it get there?” Bear asked.

“Portals can jumble small objects,” Papa said. “Like socks in a dryer, but sparklier.”

Pip clapped his little square feet. “Come! Come!”

So Bear, Papa, and Pip crawled carefully behind the ketchup and stepped through the square hole.

Pop!

They landed in Minecraft.

The grass was cubes. The clouds were cubes. Even the sun looked like a glowing cheese cracker.

Bear looked down at himself. “Papa! My shoes are blocks!”

Papa lifted one foot. “Mine too. Very fashionable. Very hard to tie.”

Pip led them over blocky hills to a bright diamond house. It sparkled blue-white, like frozen stars.

But in front of the door sat a grumpy chicken wearing an iron helmet.

“Bok,” said the chicken.

Pip gulped. “That is Sir Clucks-a-Lot. He thinks the house is his egg castle.”

Bear crouched kindly. “Excuse me, Sir Clucks-a-Lot. That is Pip’s house.”

“Bok,” said the chicken, which sounded a lot like no.

Papa tapped his chin. “We need to solve the problem without scaring anyone.”

Bear noticed a trail of seeds leading to a little chicken yard, but the trail stopped halfway.

“I know!” Bear said. “We finish the pattern.”

He placed seeds in a line. Papa counted softly. “One block apart. Nice engineering.”

Sir Clucks-a-Lot blinked. He followed the seeds.

Bok. Bok. Bok.

He strutted into his cozy yard and sat proudly in a nest.

Pip unlocked the diamond door. Click!

Inside, the house was beautiful. Diamond walls. Diamond couch. Diamond lamp. There was even a tiny bookshelf labeled: Creeper Feelings: Boom Is Not the Only Answer.

Pip sighed happily. “Home.”

Then the floor rumbled.

Bear grabbed Papa’s paw.

Papa knelt. “Breathe with me. In like smelling fries. Out like cooling soup.”

Bear breathed in. Bear breathed out.

The rumble stopped.

Pip blushed green. “Sorry. When I get nervous, I make rumbles.”

Bear smiled. “That happens. Sometimes my tummy rumbles when I’m nervous.”

Papa nodded. “Feelings are signals. We notice them. Then we choose what to do.”

Pip chose to put on soft slippers. No more rumbles.

The portal sparkled in the diamond kitchen.

Bear waved. “Goodnight, Pip.”

“Goodnight, Bear. Goodnight, clever Papa.”

Pop!

Bear and Papa were back at Redbird, safe in their booth. The fries arrived right then, as if the world had been politely waiting.

Bear dipped one fry. “Papa?”

“Yes, Bear?”

“Diamond houses are nice. But booths are nice too.”

Papa smiled. “Especially when the adventure ends with fries.”

Later, at home, Bear felt sleepy and warm. Papa tucked him in.

“Everyone safe?” Bear asked.

“Everyone safe,” Papa said. “Pip is home. Sir Clucks-a-Lot has his nest. And you are in your bed.”

Bear’s eyes closed.

And the room grew quiet as a soft square cloud.
