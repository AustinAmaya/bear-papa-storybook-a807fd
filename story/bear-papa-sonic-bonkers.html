Bear and Papa Go Bonkers with Sonic

Bear, Papa, and Sonic the Hedgehog launch a silly rescue mission for a runaway chili dog with sunglasses.

2 parts, played one after another — just press play.

Opening Choice

Bear and Papa were brushing teeth when a blue blur zipped past the bathroom door.

Whoooosh!

The toothpaste cap spun like a tiny UFO.

Sonic the Hedgehog skidded backward into the hallway, grinning.

"Hey, Bear! Hey, Papa! Emergency fun situation. I need two excellent helpers for one bonkers adventure."

Papa blinked. "Define bonkers."

Sonic held up three things: a golden ring, a skateboard with rocket wheels, and a chili dog wearing sunglasses.

Bear's eyes went huge.

What kind of bonkers adventure should they have? Option one: A super-speed ring chase through the neighborhood. Option two: Rocket-skateboard tricks at the park. Option three: A silly chili-dog rescue mission.

One-Shot Story

Bear pointed at the chili dog wearing sunglasses.

“That one,” he said. “Definitely that one.”

Sonic snapped his fingers. “Excellent choice. That is Captain Chili. He was supposed to be dinner, but then he rolled away with my last golden ring.”

Papa looked at the chili dog, which was now scooting down the hallway on a skateboard made from a cracker.

“I have several questions,” Papa said.

“No time!” Sonic cried. “Bonkers first. Questions later!”

Whoooosh!

Sonic zoomed after Captain Chili. Bear ran after Sonic. Papa ran after Bear, carrying a napkin like a rescue net.

Captain Chili bumped down the stairs, bounced off a shoe, and shot through the open back door into the backyard.

Bear burst outside laughing. “He’s fast for a snack!”

“He has mustard confidence,” Papa said.

Captain Chili rolled across the deck, past Little Red Bear, who stayed perfectly still but said, “That hot dog appears to be making poor choices.”

“He’s not a hot dog,” Sonic called. “He’s a chili dog with dreams!”

Captain Chili hit a pinecone ramp and flew through the air.

“Incoming!” shouted Bear.

Papa dropped to one knee and held the napkin wide. Sonic curled into a blue spin and made a tiny wind whoosh. Bear slid on his socks across the deck—safely, because Papa’s hand was right there—and tapped the cracker skateboard just enough to slow it down.

Plop.

Captain Chili landed gently in the napkin.

The golden ring popped off and rolled in a perfect circle around Bear’s feet.

Sonic caught it on one finger. “Nice move, Bear! Controlled bonkers. Best kind.”

Bear grinned. “Can bonkers be controlled?”

Papa folded the napkin around Captain Chili like a blanket. “Absolutely. Bonkers with teamwork is called an adventure.”

Sonic nodded very seriously. “And bonkers without teamwork is called losing your dinner.”

They all sat on the deck. Sonic shared backup chili dogs from his pocket, which Papa decided not to ask about. Bear gave Captain Chili one tiny crumb of bun, because even rescued food deserved a snack.

Soon Sonic zipped away in a blue streak, waving the golden ring.

Inside, Papa tucked Bear into bed.

“Papa?” Bear whispered.

“Yes, Bear?”

“That was the silliest rescue ever.”

Papa smiled. “And you handled it beautifully.”

Bear closed his eyes, still giggling softly.

Somewhere far off, Sonic called, “Gotta go sleepy!”

And Bear did.

*The End.*
