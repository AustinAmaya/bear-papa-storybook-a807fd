Bear and Papa Meet a Cheetah in the Space Station

Bear was already in pajamas when Papa brought out the telescope.

Chapter 1: The Starry Button

Bear was already in pajamas when Papa brought out the telescope.

The night was soft and dark blue. Little Red Bear sat happily on the deck rail, watching the stars as if he had all the time in the world. Little Polar Bear stood by the garden path, keeping watch over the quiet neighborhood.

Bear pressed his nose near the telescope.

“I see a tiny shining dot,” Bear whispered.

“That,” said Papa, kneeling beside him, “is the space station.”

Bear’s blue eyes went wide. “Are there snacks up there?”

Papa smiled. “Almost certainly.”

Just then, the telescope made a polite little *ping*.

A silver button popped out of its side.

Bear blinked. “Papa. Telescopes do not usually grow buttons.”

“No,” Papa said. “That is unusual telescope behavior.”

The button glowed with tiny letters:

VISIT MODE: COZY

Bear and Papa looked at each other.

“Cozy is my favorite kind of mode,” said Bear.

So Bear pressed the button.

The deck shimmered. The stars gently whooshed closer. There was no bump, no scary blast, only a warm humming sound, like a washing machine singing a lullaby.

When the shimmer faded, Bear and Papa were floating inside a bright space station.

Bear’s pajama toes drifted upward.

“Papa,” Bear said, very calmly, “my feet have become confused.”

Papa held a silver handrail. “That is called microgravity. It feels like floating because everything is falling around Earth together.”

Bear thought about that.

“So the space station is falling…but missing the ground?”

“Exactly,” said Papa.

From around the corner came a blur of golden spots.

Whoosh!

A cheetah in a blue astronaut vest zipped past upside down.

“Hello!” she called. “I’m Captain Pippa. Sorry, can’t stop. My snack drawer has escaped!”

Chapter 2: Captain Pippa’s Problem

Bear pushed gently from the wall and floated after Papa.

Captain Pippa the cheetah was the fastest mover Bear had ever seen. Even in space, she seemed speedy. Her tail curled behind her like a question mark.

A small drawer full of crackers, fruit pouches, and one very proud banana floated down the hallway.

“It was supposed to stay closed,” Pippa said. “But the latch went *click-flip-boing*, and now snack time is orbiting the hallway.”

Bear giggled. “The banana looks like it is captain now.”

Papa caught the banana with two fingers. “A very curved captain.”

Bear noticed little strips on the walls. “What are those fuzzy patches?”

“Velcro,” said Pippa. “Very useful in space. If you do not stick things down, they wander off and become mysterious.”

Bear nodded. “Like socks at home.”

“Exactly like socks,” Papa said.

They gathered the crackers. They caught the fruit pouches. But the drawer itself floated toward a round air vent.

“Oh no,” Pippa said. “If it blocks the vent, the station’s bedtime air will get all swirly in the wrong place.”

Bear looked at the vent. He could feel a gentle breeze pulling the drawer closer.

“How do we stop it?” he asked.

Papa spoke softly. “In space, little pushes matter. If we push too hard, we zoom too far.”

Bear took a breath. “Small pushes.”

“Small pushes,” Papa agreed.

Captain Pippa handed Bear a soft magnetic grabber. “For careful helpers.”

Bear held it proudly. He pushed off the wall with one tiny tap.

He floated slowly.

Very slowly.

“Papa,” he said, “I am a space turtle.”

“A very excellent space turtle,” Papa said.

Bear reached out the grabber.

Click.

The drawer stuck to it.

But then the air vent gave one last puff, and Bear began drifting backward with the drawer.

“Wheeee,” said Bear. Then, less wheeee, “Papa?”

“I’ve got you,” Papa said.

Papa caught Bear’s pajama sleeve. Pippa caught Papa’s belt loop. Together they made a floating chain.

The drawer was safe.

The vent was clear.

Captain Pippa clapped. Her claps made her spin gently in a circle.

“Best rescue team in orbit,” she said.

Chapter 3: The Cozy Orbit

Now there was only one problem.

The snack drawer latch was still broken.

“If it pops open again,” Pippa said, “we’ll have cracker comets all night.”

Bear looked at the latch. He looked at the Velcro. He looked at Papa.

“Could we make a helper strap?” Bear asked.

Papa’s eyes twinkled. “That sounds like engineering.”

Bear loved that word.

Captain Pippa brought a soft strap, two sticky Velcro squares, and a tiny space screwdriver. Bear held the strap in place. Papa tightened the little screws. Pippa tested it with one gentle tug.

The drawer stayed closed.

Pippa gasped. “Snack security restored.”

Bear gave the drawer a serious look. “No more escaping. It is bedtime.”

The drawer did not answer, which was polite.

Captain Pippa invited them to the station window. Earth glowed below, blue and white and quiet. Clouds curled like cream in cocoa.

Bear floated between Papa and Pippa.

“It looks peaceful,” Bear whispered.

“It is home,” Papa said.

Pippa nodded. “And home looks extra beautiful when you are far away.”

Bear yawned. It was a big yawn. The kind that starts in your toes and makes your eyes feel warm.

Papa gently tucked Bear under one arm so he would not drift.

The silver button shimmered again.

“Thank you for helping,” said Captain Pippa. “And thank you for saving the banana captain.”

Bear smiled sleepily. “Tell it to be responsible.”

“I will,” Pippa promised.

The station hummed. The stars softened. The deck returned beneath them, steady and familiar.

Little Red Bear still sat happily on the rail. Little Polar Bear still watched the garden path. The telescope was just a telescope again.

Papa carried Bear inside.

“Small pushes matter,” Bear murmured.

“They do,” Papa said. “In space, and sometimes at bedtime too.”

Bear snuggled under his blanket. Papa sat close for a moment, warm and quiet.

Outside, the space station crossed the sky like a tiny silver seed.

Inside, everything was safe.

Bear closed his eyes.

And the whole house drifted gently into sleep.
